SCHEME: PEKA B40 (Skim Peduli Kesihatan untuk Kumpulan B40)
SOURCE: https://protecthealth.com.my/peka-b40-eng/ | www.pekab40.com.my
---
ELIGIBILITY:
- Malaysian citizen aged 40 years and above
- Must be a Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR) recipient OR the spouse of a STR recipient
- No pre-registration required — just bring MyKad to any PeKa B40-registered clinic

BENEFIT 1 — FREE HEALTH SCREENING:
- Blood glucose, blood pressure, cholesterol, BMI check
- Urine dipstick test
- Mental health screening
- Breast examination (women) and prostate screening (men)
- Completely free, no fasting needed

BENEFIT 2 — MEDICAL EQUIPMENT ASSISTANCE (up to RM20,000):
- Hearing aids, wheelchairs, artificial limbs (prostheses)
- Pacemakers, heart stents, cochlear implants
- Must complete Benefit 1 (health screening) first

BENEFIT 3 — CANCER TREATMENT INCENTIVE (RM1,000 total):
- RM300 paid when cancer treatment begins
- RM700 paid after treatment is completed
- Applies to confirmed cancer diagnosis at government hospital

BENEFIT 4 — TRANSPORT INCENTIVE:
- Peninsular Malaysia: up to RM500
- Sabah, Sarawak, Labuan: up to RM1,000
- For recipients of Benefit 2 or Benefit 3 only

HOW TO ACCESS:
- Walk in to any PeKa B40 panel clinic with your MyKad
- No appointment needed for Benefit 1
- List of panel clinics: www.pekab40.com.my

SCHEME: mySALAM (National Health Protection Scheme)
SOURCE: https://www.mysalam.com.my
CARELINE: 1-300-888-938
---
WHAT IT IS:
- FREE national health takaful scheme — no premiums, no deductions
- Government-funded protection for B40 and lower M40 Malaysians

ELIGIBILITY (AUTOMATIC — B40):
- Malaysian citizen aged 18 to 65
- Registered recipient of: STR, BSH, BPR, or BKM
- Auto-enrolled — no action needed

ELIGIBILITY (VOLUNTARY — M40):
- Annual taxable income RM100,000 and below
- Must register voluntarily at mysalam.com.my

BENEFIT 1 — CRITICAL ILLNESS PAYOUT (RM8,000):
- One-time cash payment upon diagnosis of any of 50 critical illnesses
- Covered illnesses include: Cancer, Stroke, Heart Attack, Kidney Failure, Alzheimer's Disease, Parkinson's Disease, Blindness, Deafness, Major Burns, Coma, and 40 more
- Paid directly into recipient's bank account

BENEFIT 2 — HOSPITALISATION ALLOWANCE (RM50/day):
- Maximum 14 days per year
- Must be admitted as inpatient (overnight in ward)
- At government hospitals, military hospitals, or university hospitals only
- Private hospital admissions are NOT covered

HOW TO CLAIM — CRITICAL ILLNESS:
1. Get diagnosis confirmed by a doctor at a government hospital
2. Doctor fills in mySALAM Critical Illness Form
3. Submit form + documents online at mysalam.com.my OR at mySALAM kiosks in public hospitals

HOW TO CLAIM — HOSPITALISATION:
1. Get hospital discharge summary (Ringkasan Discaj)
2. Submit at mysalam.com.my or mySALAM kiosk within 60 days of discharge

DOCUMENTS NEEDED:
- MyKad (front and back)
- Hospital discharge note or specialist letter
- Bank account details (must match MyKad name)

SCHEME: TABUNG BANTUAN PERUBATAN (TBP) — Medical Assistance Fund
SOURCE: Ministry of Health Malaysia — www.moh.gov.my
---
WHAT IT IS:
- Government fund managed by MOH to help patients who CANNOT afford treatment at government hospitals
- Means-tested — based on financial hardship

ELIGIBILITY:
- Malaysian citizen
- Currently a patient at a government/public hospital
- Unable to pay outstanding medical bills (financially qualified)
- Assessed by Medical Social Work Unit at the hospital

WHAT IT COVERS:
- Outstanding hospital bills at government hospitals
- Dialysis treatment costs
- Cancer treatment (chemotherapy, radiotherapy)
- Specialist equipment and implants
- Referral and transport assistance in some cases

HOW TO APPLY:
1. Go to the Medical Social Work Unit (Unit Kerja Sosial Perubatan) at the government hospital where you are being treated
2. Speak to the Medical Social Officer (Pegawai Kerja Sosial Perubatan)
3. Bring: MyKad, proof of income (payslip, EPF statement, or statutory declaration), outstanding hospital bills, and referral letter from doctor if available
4. The officer will assess your case and submit on your behalf

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- You do NOT apply online — must go in person to the hospital
- Decision is made by a hospital committee
- Amount and type of assistance varies per case

SCHEME: SKIM PERUBATAN MADANI (Free Clinic Treatment)
SOURCE: https://protecthealth.com.my/skimperubatanmadani-ms/ | www.moh.gov.my
---
WHAT IT IS:
- Free treatment for MINOR illnesses at registered government panel clinics
- For STR recipients — auto-eligible, no registration needed

ELIGIBILITY:
- Must be a registered STR recipient (Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah)
- Three categories:
  a) Household recipients: RM250 credit per year
  b) Senior citizens without partner: RM125 credit per year
  c) Singles: RM75 credit per year

WHAT IS COVERED (minor illnesses only):
- Fever, flu, cough, sore throat
- Diarrhoea, vomiting, stomach ache
- Headache, dizziness
- Minor sprains and wounds
- Skin rashes (minor)

WHAT IS NOT COVERED:
- Major surgeries, hospitalisation, specialist treatment
- Chronic disease management (hypertension, diabetes follow-up)
- Dental and optical treatment

CURRENTLY AVAILABLE IN 10 DISTRICTS:
Kuala Lumpur, Gombak, Hulu Langat, Petaling, Klang (Selangor), Johor Bahru, Kinta (Perak), Timur Laut (Penang), Kota Kinabalu (Sabah), Kuching (Sarawak)

HOW TO ACCESS:
- Walk in to any Skim Perubatan Madani panel clinic in the listed districts
- Bring MyKad — receptionist will verify STR status
- Treatment deducted from your annual credit

SCHEME: SOCSO / PERKESO (Social Security Organisation)
SOURCE: www.perkeso.gov.my
CARELINE: 1-300-22-8000
---
WHAT IT IS:
- Statutory body protecting EMPLOYEES against work-related accidents and occupational diseases
- Managed by PERKESO (Pertubuhan Keselamatan Sosial)

WHO IS COVERED:
- All employees earning RM5,000/month or less — MANDATORY coverage
- Employees earning above RM5,000 can opt in voluntarily
- Both employer AND employee contribute monthly

WHO IS NOT COVERED:
- Self-employed individuals (unless voluntarily registered under SKSPS scheme)
- Domestic helpers
- Civil servants (covered under PERKESO since 2019 — check with employer)

EMPLOYMENT INJURY SCHEME (EIS) BENEFITS:

1. MEDICAL BENEFIT — UNLIMITED
   - Free treatment at SOCSO panel clinics and hospitals
   - Covers all costs for work-related injuries: consultations, surgery, medication, physiotherapy

2. TEMPORARY DISABILITY BENEFIT (TDB):
   - 80% of daily wage for each day unable to work
   - Paid from Day 1 of medical leave
   - Continues until worker recovers or is assessed for permanent disability

3. PERMANENT DISABILITY BENEFIT (PDB):
   - Monthly pension if injury results in permanent disability
   - Amount based on % of disability assessed by SOCSO medical board

4. DEATH/SURVIVORS' PENSION:
   - Monthly pension to dependants (spouse, children) if worker dies from work accident
   - Dependent child benefit until age 21

5. RETURN TO WORK (RTW) PROGRAMME:
   - Rehabilitation, retraining, and job placement support
   - For workers who cannot return to their original job

HOW TO CLAIM AFTER A WORK ACCIDENT:
1. Inform your EMPLOYER immediately (within 48 hours)
2. Employer submits Accident Report (Borang 34) to SOCSO within 48 hours
3. You seek treatment at a SOCSO panel clinic or hospital
4. Bring your MyKad and SOCSO card (or show employer contribution record)
5. All treatment costs billed directly to SOCSO — you pay nothing

FOR OCCUPATIONAL DISEASE:
- Doctor must certify the disease is work-related
- Submit Form 34A through employer

ONLINE PORTAL: https://www.perkeso.gov.my/en/our-services/protection/employment-injury-scheme
